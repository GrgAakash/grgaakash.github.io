SIHR Model: System of ODEs

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{dS}{dt} &= -p_1 \beta S I \\ \frac{dI}{dt} &= p_1 \beta S I - p_2 \gamma I \\ \frac{dH}{dt} &= p_2 p_h \gamma I - p_3 \alpha H \\ \frac{dR}{dt} &= p_2 (1-p_h) \gamma I + p_3 \alpha H \end{align*} \]

  • S: Susceptible
  • I: Infected
  • H: Hospitalized
  • R: Recovered
  • β, γ, α: rates
  • p₁, p₂, p₃, pₕ: probabilities

Hospitalization Pattern Classification

The SIHR model's hospitalized population \( h(t) \) exhibits diminishing (monotonically decreasing), viral (initially increasing, then decreasing after a peak), or outbreak (initially decreasing, then increasing to a peak, then decreasing) behavior, determined by:

\[ \sigma_0 = s_0 \frac{\beta}{\gamma}, \quad \tilde{\sigma}_0 = \frac{\gamma p_{IH} i_0}{\alpha h_0}, \quad \tilde{\tilde{\sigma}}_0 = \frac{\gamma p_{IH} i(t_{p,i})}{\alpha h(t_{p,i})} \] where \( t_{p,i} \) is the peak time of \( i \).

Now we categorize the outcomes of \( h \) as:

\[ \begin{aligned} &\textbf{If } h_0 = 0: &&\quad \boxed{\text{viral}} \\[10pt] &\textbf{If } h_0 > 0: && \quad \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \sigma_0 \leq 1: & \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \tilde{\sigma}_0 \geq 1 &\Rightarrow \text{viral} \\ \tilde{\sigma}_0 < 1 &\Rightarrow \text{diminishing} \end{array} \right. \\[12pt] \sigma_0 > 1: & \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \tilde{\sigma}_0 > 1 &\Rightarrow \text{viral} \\ \tilde{\sigma}_0 \leq 1 & \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \tilde{\tilde{\sigma}}_0 < 1 &\Rightarrow \text{diminishing} \\ \tilde{\tilde{\sigma}}_0 \geq 1 &\Rightarrow \text{outbreak} \end{array} \right. \end{array} \right. \end{array} \right. \end{aligned} \]
